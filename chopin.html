Frederic chopin

Frédéric Chopin (1810–1849) was a Polish-French composer and virtuoso pianist of the Romantic era. A child prodigy, he began composing early and gave public performances by age seven. He left Poland at 20 and settled in Paris, where he spent the rest of his life. Deeply patriotic, Chopin expressed his love for Poland through music. He wrote almost exclusively for solo piano, creating technically brilliant and emotionally rich works like mazurkas, nocturnes, and polonaises. Despite chronic illness, likely tuberculosis, he composed some of his greatest music in Paris before dying at age 39. His influence on piano music remains profound.

1810
 Born in Żelazowa Wola in 1810 to a French father and a Polish mother
1816–1826
 Begins playing the piano at age 6, showing extraordinary talent. Receives instruction from Wojciech Żywny and starts composing short piano pieces.
1829
 Travels to Vienna for the first time, performing to enthusiastic audiences. Composes the Piano Concerto in F minor, among other works.
1830
 Leaves Poland around the time of the November Uprising against Russia. Moves to Vienna, then soon relocates to Paris, where he spends most of his life.
1831–1835
 Gains recognition in Paris as a pianist and composer. Forms friendships with figures like Franz Liszt, Hector Berlioz, and painter Eugène Delacroix. Composes works such as Études Op. 10, Nocturnes, and Ballades.
1836
 Meets French novelist George Sand (pen name of Aurore Dudevant), beginning a long personal and artistic relationship.
1838–1839
 Spends winter in Majorca with George Sand; falls ill, likely with tuberculosis. Despite health struggles, he composes several important pieces, including the Preludes, Op. 28.
1839–1846
 Lives part-time at George Sand’s estate in Nohant, where he produces many of his greatest works, including polonaises, waltzes, and sonatas.
1847
 His relationship with George Sand ends, and his health continues to worsen.
1848
 Makes a final tour of England and Scotland, performing in London and Edinburgh. Despite his poor condition, he gives several concerts, including one for Queen Victoria.
1849
 Dies on October 17 in Paris at age 39. Buried at Père Lachaise Cemetery; his heart is returned to Warsaw, as he requested.
